English

Throughout the year, English lessons have given us the opportunity to communicate in a different language in meaningful contexts, especially those related to computer
science, but also in cultural and historical discussions. This kind of preparation is particularly useful in case we pursue work abroad or find ourselves in international
environments. It also allowed us to deepen our understanding of key events in modern history. In particular, we studied the Cold War and one of its early episodes: the
Berlin Blockade. On June 24, 1948, the Soviet Union blocked all access to West Berlin in response to the introduction of the Deutschmark in West Germany and the launch
of the U.S. Marshall Plan aimed at countering communism. As a response, NATO member countries organized an airlift to supply West Berlin with essential goods,
bypassing the Soviet blockade.
